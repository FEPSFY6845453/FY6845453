Abstract

        Distributed generation has gained a very important role in the world of electrical engineering
        in the recent years. To briefly explain distributed generation (DG) involves generating power
        locally from small scale, mainly renewable energy sources near the end user as opposed to
        a central power plant Several potential benefits of distributed generation include increased
        energy efficiency in contrast to widely used renewable energy methods, reduce power loss
        during transmission, improved grid reliability and the ability to combine renewable energy
        sources. However distributed generation also poses a number. of challenges that need to be
        solved for the most efficient and safe way to use distributed generation. This article
        investigates some of these challenges and provides an overview into the technical and
        environmental aspects of distributed generation.

Introduction

        Distributed generators (DG) are defined as the plant which is directly connected to
        the distribution network and is not centrally planned and dispatched. These are alsof
        called embedded or dispersed generation units. interconnection of these generators
        will offer anumber.of benefits such as improved reliability, power quality, efficiency,
        alleviation of system constraintsi along with he environmental benefits (Gaonkar,
        D.N. (2010) Distributed generation.) Unlike centralized power plants, which supply
        electricity to a large area, distributed generation systems generate power on a
        smaller scale and typically serve a local community or building. These energy
        sources can include renewable energy sources like solar panels, wind turbines, as
        well as traditional sources like natural gas generators. The main characteristic of
        distributed generation is its ability to provide electricity closer to the end consumer,
        reducing the need for long- distance transmission and distribution of power.
        (Distributed Generation of Electricity and its Environmental Impacts | US EPA (2023).)
