Abstract

        The concept of distributed generation in
electrical engineering has gained significant importance in. recent years This approach involves generating electricity from multiple small-scale
energy sources located close to the end users, as opposed to a centralized power plant. Distributed generation offers several potential benefits, such as
increased energy efficiency, reduced transmission losses, improved grid reliability, and the ability to integrate renewable energy sources. However, it
also presents several challenges that need to be addressed for its successful implementation. This article explores these challenges and provides insights into the technical, economic, and
environmental aspects of distributed generation.
